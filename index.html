FAQ

Does this work on time‑based charts?

It’s designed for Renko bar formation timing. On time‑based charts, “bar duration” is fixed by the timeframe, so the indicator is most useful on non‑time‑based bars like Renko.

Why do some bars show very small or very large times?

Renko bars form faster during high volatility and slower during low volatility. Session transitions and illiquid periods can also create longer formation times.

Does it repaint?

The live (currently forming) bar’s time will change until that bar completes. Once a bar closes, its measured formation duration is fixed.

How can I use this for trade filtering?

Many traders use formation time as a volatility proxy—filtering trades when bars are forming unusually fast (high momentum) or unusually slow (chop/low energy).

Will it reset correctly across overnight sessions?

Yes—session‑aware logic is intended to prevent bar‑time carryover across session boundaries. Your chart’s Trading Hours template still matters.

What unit should I use?

Seconds are a common default. Use milliseconds for very fast markets/bars, or minutes/hours when bars can take a long time to form.

Does it impact performance?

It updates on each tick to keep the timing accurate. On very high‑tick instruments, that can be heavier than OnBarClose indicators, but it’s typically lightweight.

Can I change where the chart time text appears?

Yes—enable the optional chart‑time display and choose the corner location in settings.
